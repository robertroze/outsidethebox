Some young people don't fit the system. We're built for them.

Outside the Box is a new CIC helping young people aged 14 to 24 in Nuneaton and Bedworth gather skills, build independence and gain employment. Not a school, not a job centre — like the young people we work with, we're built different.

Get involved How it works
What we do

We're not a school. We're not a job centre. We're something different.

We're collaborating with volunteer mentors, SEN and mental-health specialists and work coaches to build a scheme that gives young people the bright start they deserve. We find out what each person is genuinely good at and build a route forward that fits them — not one designed for someone else.

Our whole framework is built on self-reliance. We don't hand out short-term fixes — we give young people the operational toolkit to manage their own independent lives, and the confidence to focus on their own achievements as the building blocks of everything that comes next.

The pilot

A live proof of concept.

The pilot runs as short courses that test our course material and demonstrate the impact we can have — so we can on-board local businesses willing to give our young people a role in their company.

Training runs in six-week blocks of 6–10 students. We engage the most marginalised and disengaged young people in the borough first, then enrol them onto the wider scheme of formal qualifications and work placements.

We run from our premises in Nuneaton and hire appropriate community spaces nearby, so travel is never the thing that stops someone taking part.

Why this matters

There's only a small window to reach these young people.

6.2%

under-25 claimant rate in Nuneaton & Bedworth — the highest in Warwickshire.

+13.5%

rise in the Warwickshire youth claimant count in a single year — concentrated in the borough's post-industrial north.

13.5%

of all 16–24 year-olds are now classified as NEET nationally — and community estimates put the borough's hidden figure higher still.

Where there's no work, young people look for other — often antisocial — activities, and that becomes a generational cycle of disengagement. It can lead to mental-health issues, social isolation, addiction and criminality. These are among the poorest wards in Warwickshire and the UK, so the pressure on 16–24 year-olds here is far more acute than in neighbouring areas like Stratford or Warwick.

About us

Built to fill the gap, not replace what's already there.

Who we are

A community interest company based in Nuneaton and Bedworth. We were set up because we kept seeing the same thing: young people who were switched off, written off, or just didn't fit what was on offer — and very little in between to catch them.

We're not trying to replace what already exists. We're trying to fill the gap it leaves.

How we work

We build individual packages — understanding what a young person can do, finding employers genuinely open to taking someone on, and matching the two properly. Not just sending a CV and hoping.

Our mentors are chosen for lived experience as well as trade skills. They know what it's like to not have a straight path. That matters.

Where we are

We work in Nuneaton and Bedworth. That's intentional. These are our communities and we know them.

We're a pilot at this stage. Small, deliberate, and focused on getting it right before we grow it.

For young people & families

For employers

We're looking for local employers willing to be honest with us — about what skills you actually need, what puts you off a candidate, and what would make you take a chance on someone.

We're not asking you to take anyone on straightaway. We're asking you to help us understand what "work-ready" really means in your business.

For funders & partners

We're not promising to solve everything. We're promising to do this properly, learn from it, and be transparent about what works.
